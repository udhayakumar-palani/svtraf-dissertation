**Conclusion:** SVTRAF significantly outperforms all baselines on correlation with ground truth (financial losses).

### 2. Ranking Quality (NDCG@10)

Top 10 vulnerabilities correctly ranked by SVTRAF with 99.88% ideal ranking quality.

- SVTRAF ranks the highest-loss contracts correctly
- Minimal difference from CVSS (0.9988 vs 0.9878) but more significant gap vs BVSS/Ahmad

---

### 3. Correlation with Ground Truth (Spearman ρ)

**SVTRAF: ρ = 0.9636** (p < 0.001)
- Strong, highly significant monotonic correlation with actual financial losses
- 27.9% improvement over CVSS (ρ = 0.7537)
- Indicates SVTRAF scores reliably predict loss magnitude

**CVSS v3.1: ρ = 0.7537** (p < 0.001)
- Moderate positive correlation; misses blockchain-specific factors
- Standard deviation: σ = 0.0821

**BVSS: ρ = 0.6284** (p < 0.001)
- Lower correlation; less comprehensive modeling

---

### 4. Prediction Accuracy (MAE)

| Framework | Mean Absolute Error | Interpretation |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **SVTRAF** | **0.402** | Average prediction off by 0.4 score points |
| CVSS v3.1 | 1.347 | Average prediction off by 1.3 score points |
| BVSS | 1.891 | Average prediction off by 1.9 score points |
| Ahmad et al. | 1.654 | Average prediction off by 1.7 score points |

**SVTRAF achieves 70.1% lower error than CVSS v3.1**

---

### 5. Statistical Significance (Wilcoxon Test)

**Null hypothesis:** SVTRAF and CVSS have equivalent effect on loss prediction

**Result:** p < 0.001 — **Reject null hypothesis**

- Highly significant difference (α = 0.05)
- SVTRAF significantly better predicts losses than CVSS
- Robust to non-normal distributions

---

**Practical significance:** Beyond statistical significance, effect sizes are large, indicating SVTRAF provides meaningfully better predictions.

## 9. Implications

1. **SVTRAF is blockchain-specific:** Immutability (AMP component) is critical to scoring accuracy
2. **Financial damage modeling works:** ISC component captures real-world loss magnitude better than generic metrics
3. **CVSS is insufficient for smart contracts:** 27.9% worse correlation indicates need for domain-specific frameworks
4. **Framework is robust:** Large effect sizes demonstrate practical significance beyond statistical tests

---

## 10. Limitations

- **Ethereum/Solidity focus:** Framework tested on EVM-compatible contracts; generalization to other chains unclear
- **Documented exploits only:** Analysis covers contracts with *known* losses; unknown exploits not captured
- **Static snapshot:** Dataset represents 2018–2024; threat landscape evolves
- **Stratification bias:** Equal category distribution (5 × 30) may not reflect real-world vulnerability distribution
